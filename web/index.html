Foreign Students at Korean Universities

About this map & data sources

This site maps foreign students and residents in South Korea (2024–2025), with a focus on people from Muslim-majority countries (countries where more than 50% of the population is Muslim). All student and resident counts come directly from official South Korean government statistics.

Government statistical sources

  • University students (the “All foreign students”, “Muslim-country students”, and “Muslim students by province” views): the official university information disclosure system 대학알리미 (Academy Info), operated under the Ministry of Education and compiled by the Korea Educational Development Institute (KEDI). Disclosure item “Foreign Student Status (외국학생 현황)”, 2025 survey year (as of 1 April 2025). Mirrored on data.go.kr.
  • Registered residents (the “Muslim residents by province” view): Ministry of Justice — Korea Immigration Service, Statistical Yearbook of Immigration & Foreigner Policy 2024 (출입국·외국인정책 통계연보 2024), table “Registered foreigners by district and nationality”. Figures as of 31 December 2024.

Supporting data (not statistics)

  • University map locations: OpenStreetMap (Nominatim) — approximate coordinates, for placing markers only.
  • Province boundaries: the open southkorea-maps dataset, derived from Statistics Korea (KOSTAT) administrative boundaries.
  • “Muslim-majority” classification: our own list of countries with a Muslim population above 50%.

Notes

  • Mongolia is not a Muslim-majority country; where shown it is labelled “non-Muslim” and is excluded from every Muslim-country total.
  • Fully online (“cyber”) universities are omitted from the campus maps as they have no physical location.

In short: the numbers are official Korean government data (Ministry of Education / KEDI and Ministry of Justice, 2024–2025). Coordinates, boundary shapes, and the Muslim-majority definition are open data or editorial choices.
